# Bank Marketing Campaign Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes customer engagement factors in bank marketing campaigns using the Bank Marketing dataset.

The goal was to identify which factors most influence whether a customer subscribes to a term deposit. The analysis focuses on customer contact duration, demographic characteristics, and campaign timing and communication type.

## Objectives

### 1. Does contact duration affect subscription success?
This objective examines whether the duration of the last customer contact influences the likelihood of subscribing to a term deposit.

### 2. How do demographic factors affect loan uptake?
This objective analyzes how age, marital status, and education influence whether a customer has taken a loan.

### 3. What is the impact of contact timing and communication type on campaign success?
This objective evaluates how contact method, campaign frequency, timing, and previous interactions affect the success of marketing campaigns.

## Tools Used

- R
- ggplot2
- dplyr
- randomForest
- Logistic Regression

## Key Findings

- Longer contact duration was positively associated with a higher probability of subscription.
- Younger customers were more likely to take loans than older customers.
- Education level and marital status also influenced loan uptake.
- Contact type, campaign timing, and number of campaign contacts affected subscription success.
- December and March showed stronger campaign success than some other months.
